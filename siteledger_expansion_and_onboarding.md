# SiteLedger: Expansion & India-Specific Onboarding Architecture

This document outlines the strategic pathways for expanding SiteLedger into new real estate paradigms, alongside a comprehensive, **India-specific** data checklist required when onboarding a new Developer/Construction Company to the platform.

---

## Part 1: Expanding SiteLedger's Project Capabilities

SiteLedger is currently optimized for **Residential Multi-floor Buildings (Apartments/Flats)**. Below are the key project paradigms we can expand into, why they differ, and what new inputs they require.

### 1. Plotted Development & Gated Communities (Horizontal)
Instead of building upward, the developer acquires a massive tract of land, develops the infrastructure, and sells individual land plots or pre-built villas.
*   **Why it's different:** Progress is measured in civil infrastructure (roads laid, sewage treatment plants, streetlights, boundary walls). Inventory is horizontal.
*   **New Inputs to Capture:**
    *   `Total Land Area (Acres/Guntas/Sq.Yards)` and `Survey Numbers/Khata Numbers`.
    *   `Infrastructure Milestones` (e.g., % of road paved, underground cabling status).
    *   `Plot Dimensions` (e.g., 30x40, 40x60, corner plots vs. standard plots).
    *   `Approval Authorities` (e.g., BDA/BMRDA in Bangalore, HMDA in Hyderabad, DTCP, etc.).

### 2. Commercial Real Estate (Office Spaces & Retail Malls)
Building structures meant for businesses, retail shops, or corporate offices.
*   **Why it's different:** The developer leases out "Carpet Area" to corporate tenants rather than selling 100 different flats to homeowners. Costs heavily revolve around HVAC, high-speed elevators, and "Tenant Fit-outs".
*   **New Inputs to Capture:**
    *   `Gross Leasable Area (GLA)` vs. `Carpet Area` (Square feet/meters).
    *   `CAM (Common Area Maintenance) Rates` (e.g., ₹10/sqft/month).
    *   `Tenant Fit-out Budgets` (Tracking expenses specific to a tenant's request).
    *   `HVAC Tonnage` and `Parking Allocation` (e.g., 1 parking spot per 1000 sq ft).

### 3. Government Infrastructure / Contracting (Roads, Bridges)
Taking on government tenders (like PWD, NHAI, or CPWD) to build public infrastructure.
*   **Why it's different:** The customer is a single entity (the Government). Revenue is generated by submitting **"RA Bills" (Running Account Bills)** as milestones are completed. The government holds back "Retention Money" and requires "Bank Guarantees."
*   **New Inputs to Capture:**
    *   `Tender ID` and `Project Value`.
    *   `RA Bill Tracking` (Bill submitted, certified, passed, pending).
    *   `EMD (Earnest Money Deposit)` and `PBG (Performance Bank Guarantee)` tracking.
    *   `Retention Percentage` (e.g., 5% withheld until the Defect Liability Period ends).

## Part 2: India-Specific Developer Company Onboarding Checklist

When a new real estate or construction company registers on SiteLedger in India, capturing the right legal, financial, and taxation metadata is critical. India has strict real estate and tax laws (RERA, GST, TDS). These details feed directly into automated invoices, customer receipts, and compliance reports.

### 1. Basic Identity & Branding
*   **Legal Company Name:** As registered on their MCA (Ministry of Corporate Affairs) incorporation certificate.
*   **Trade Name (DBA):** The brand name they actually sell under.
*   **Company Logo:** High-resolution image (Used for printing on customer receipts, vendor POs).
*   **Registered Office Address:** Essential for tax authorities.
*   **Corporate/Site Office Address:** For general correspondence.

### 2. Taxation & Compliance Identifiers (Mandatory in India)
*   **GSTIN (Goods and Services Tax ID):** Absolutely mandatory for B2B billing (Input Tax Credit) and printing on vendor/customer receipts. You need to know if they are charging 1%, 5%, or 12% GST based on affordable vs non-affordable housing.
*   **PAN (Permanent Account Number):** The company's corporate tax ID.
*   **TAN (Tax Deduction and Collection Account Number):** Crucial. Construction companies deduct massive amounts of TDS (Tax Deducted at Source) when paying contractors and vendors. SiteLedger must track TDS deducted.
*   **CIN (Corporate Identification Number):** Issued by MCA for Pvt Ltd / Public Ltd companies.
*   **MSME / Udyam Registration No:** To track if they fall under the MSME payment act (vendors must be paid within 45 days).
*   **EPF & ESIC Registration Numbers:** Employee Provident Fund and State Insurance numbers. Heavily scrutinized in construction due to contract labor laws.
*   **BOCW Registration (Building and Other Construction Workers):** Specifically for tracking Labour Cess (usually 1% of the construction cost paid to the government).

### 3. Real Estate Regulatory Authority (RERA) Compliance
*   **RERA Registration Number:** *Must* be printed on all customer-facing documents, receipts, booking forms, and marketing materials.
*   **Project RERA Approvals:** Each specific site needs its own RERA ID and validity end-date (tracking project deadlines).

### 4. Financial & Banking Configurations (The Escrow Rule)
*   **Master Bank Account Details:** Account Name, Number, IFSC code, and Branch.
*   **RERA Escrow Account (70% Rule):** Under the RERA Act, 2016, a developer must deposit 70% of the money collected from customers into a designated Escrow account. This money can *only* be used for construction costs and land cost for that specific project. SiteLedger must allow companies to map which bank account is the "RERA Escrow Account".
*   **Authorized Signatories:** Names and designations of the people authorized to sign receipts and vendor checks. (Can be used to generate digital signatures).

### 5. Document Numbering & Operational Formatting
*   **Financial Year Configuration:** In India, this is strictly **April 1st to March 31st**. SiteLedger must automatically partition ledgers by FY (e.g., FY 23-24, FY 24-25).
*   **Receipt / Invoice Prefix Config:** Allow them to set custom prefixes for documents for different FYs (e.g., `SL/REC/24-25/001`, `PRJ/INV/24-25/001`).
*   **Terms & Conditions Boilerplate:** Legal disclaimers appended to the bottom of receipts (e.g., "Subject to local jurisdiction", "Payment is subject to realization of cheque").
